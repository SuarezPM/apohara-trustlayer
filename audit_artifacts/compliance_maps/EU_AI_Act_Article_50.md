## Article 50(1)(a) — Visible disclosure to natural persons

> "Providers of AI systems [...] shall ensure that the AI system is designed and developed in such a way that [...] the affected natural persons are informed that they are interacting with an AI system."

## Article 50(2) — Machine-readable marking

> "[...] the output of the AI system shall be marked in a machine-readable format and detectable as artificially generated or manipulated."

### TrustLayer implementation
- **File:** `crates/tl-evidence/src/cose.rs::CoseSignature::ed25519` (sign) + `verify` (verify)
- **Output field:** `SignedReceipt.cose_sign1_b64` (base64-encoded COSE_Sign1 envelope)

- **Default:** `Compliant` if `TL_TSA_PROVIDER` is `free_tsa` or `digicert`; `Partial` (with missing=["RFC 3161 timestamp authority is mock (non-forensic)"]) if `mock`.

## Code of Practice §3.2 — Multi-layer marking

The Code of Practice (10 June 2026) requires that AI-generated content be marked via **multiple layers**:
1. Digitally signed and time-stamped metadata (COSE_Sign1 + RFC 3161)
2. Imperceptible watermarking (v1.1: Kirchenbauer / Tree-Ring / AudioSeal)

### TrustLayer implementation
The 4-layer `ComplianceAssessment` is the concrete implementation of this multi-layer requirement:
- Layer 1: visible disclosure (per Art. 50(1)(a))
- Layer 2: machine-readable provenance (COSE_Sign1 + RFC 3161, per Art. 50(2))
- Layer 3: watermark (per Art. 50(3); v1 reports `NotApplicable` honestly)
- Layer 4: retention with tamper-evidence (per Art. 50(4))

**`disclaimers` field in every response surfaces which layers are `Compliant` vs `Partial` vs `NotApplicable` in v1** (AC-22 anti-greenwashing).
